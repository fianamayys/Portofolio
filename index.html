mise-en-scène

I grew up with a deep interest in the world of art and storytelling. One of the figures who inspired me the most was Claude Monet, an impressionist artist who was able to capture the beauty of light and atmosphere with soft yet meaningful strokes. Monet's way of seeing the world was not just an object, but as an emotion that lives in color and shape, making me learn that every work can be a window of feelings, not just a visual result.


My love for art also combines with my hobby of reading, especially books with literary and fantasy themes. I really enjoy stories that can take me across time and space, get to know complex characters, and dive into a world full of magic. From there, I learned how the power of narrative can move the heart and shape a person's perspective on life.


On the other hand, I am also a content creator who plays games. In my opinion, the world of gaming is not just about entertainment, but also a unique creative space, a place where interaction, strategy, and storytelling come together. The game creators I follow teach me that self-expression can come in many forms—including in the ever-evolving digital world.


All of these experiences—from painting, literature, and the digital world—have shaped my identity in my work. I love the process of exploring ideas, combining visual and narrative elements, and creating something that is not only beautiful to look at, but also meaningful and can be felt.
